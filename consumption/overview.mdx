The Consumption section of the Redwood Cloud Portal gives you a clear view of how your organization is using its Redwood SaaS products. Whether you're running job executions in RunMyJobs, processing transactions in Finance Automation, or transferring files with JSCAPE, the portal tracks your activity against your contracted entitlements so you can stay informed and plan ahead.

## What consumption means for Redwood products

Consumption refers to the measurable resources your organization uses within a billing period. The specific metrics tracked depend on which Redwood products you have licensed:

| Product | Consumption metrics |
|---|---|
| **RunMyJobs** | Job executions, agent connections |
| **Finance Automation** | Transaction volume, processed documents |
| **JSCAPE** | File transfers, data transfer volume |

<Info>
  The metrics displayed in your portal reflect only the products included in your organization's contract. If you have licensed multiple Redwood products, each is tracked separately.
</Info>

## Navigate to the Consumption section

To view your consumption data:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to the portal">
    Go to [portal.redwood.cloud](https://portal.redwood.cloud/) and sign in with your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Consumption">
    In the left navigation, click **Consumption**. The section expands to show **Overview** and **Contract Details**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Overview">
    Click **Overview** to open the consumption dashboard for your organization.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Access to the Consumption section requires an admin role. If you do not see **Consumption** in the navigation, contact your organization's portal administrator.
</Note>

## Metrics tracked and displayed

The consumption dashboard surfaces the key usage data for each of your licensed products. For each metric you can see:

- **Current period usage** — How much of a given resource you have used in the current billing period.
- **Entitlement limit** — The maximum usage allowed under your contract.
- **Usage percentage** — Your current usage expressed as a percentage of your entitlement.
- **Historical trend** — A chart showing usage over recent periods so you can identify patterns.

## Understanding usage dashboards and charts

Each product's usage is presented as a combination of summary cards and trend charts.

**Summary cards** at the top of the dashboard show your current-period totals at a glance. Each card displays the metric name, the current value, and a visual indicator of how close you are to your entitlement limit.

**Trend charts** below the summary cards plot your usage over time. Use these charts to:

- Identify peak usage periods and plan capacity accordingly.
- Spot unexpected spikes that may indicate configuration issues or runaway processes.
- Validate that usage is trending within expected bounds as you approach renewal.

<Tip>
  Hover over any point on a trend chart to see the exact value for that date. You can also click and drag to zoom into a specific time range.
</Tip>

## How usage relates to your contract entitlements

Your contract defines the maximum entitlement for each metric. The Consumption dashboard maps your actual usage directly against those entitlements, so you always know where you stand.

When your usage approaches your entitlement ceiling, the summary cards change color to indicate the proximity:

- **Green** — Usage is well within your entitlement.
- **Yellow** — Usage is approaching the entitlement limit (typically above 80%).
- **Red** — Usage is at or near the entitlement limit.

For detailed information about your entitlement levels and contract terms, see [Contract Details](/consumption/contract-details).

## Filter by time period or environment

You can adjust the consumption view to focus on specific time ranges or environments.

**Time period filter** — Use the date range selector at the top of the dashboard to switch between preset periods (current month, last 3 months, last 12 months) or define a custom range.

**Environment filter** — If your organization has multiple environments (for example, production and non-production instances), use the environment selector to view consumption for a specific environment or across all environments combined.

<Tip>
  Filtering to your production environment gives the clearest picture of live usage when preparing for a contract renewal conversation with your Redwood account manager.
</Tip>

## Who can view consumption data

Access to consumption data is restricted to users with the **Admin** role in the Redwood Cloud Portal. Standard users and read-only users do not see the Consumption section.

If you need consumption data but do not have admin access, ask your organization's portal administrator to either grant you admin access or export the relevant data on your behalf.

<Info>
  For questions about your contract, entitlements, or upcoming renewal, contact your Redwood account manager directly. The portal surfaces your current contract data but does not support contract modifications.
</Info>
