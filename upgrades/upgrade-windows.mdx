Upgrade windows give you precise control over when Redwood can perform maintenance on your environments. By defining the days and times that work for your organization, you ensure that upgrades never run during business-critical periods — even when Redwood applies mandatory updates.

## What upgrade windows do

When you schedule an upgrade and select a preferred date, the portal uses your upgrade window configuration to determine the exact time the upgrade will run. The upgrade is placed at the start of the first upgrade window that falls on or after your selected date.

Without an upgrade window, upgrades default to running at the beginning of the maintenance period on your chosen date. Configuring an upgrade window adds a time constraint so maintenance only begins within your approved hours.

<Note>
  Upgrade windows are configured per environment. A window you create for your production environment does not apply to your staging or development environments unless you assign it to those as well.
</Note>

## Create an upgrade window

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to upgrade windows">
    In the left sidebar, go to **Upgrades**, then select the **Upgrade Windows** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New Upgrade Window">
    Click **New Upgrade Window** to open the configuration form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the window">
    Enter a descriptive name for the window, such as "Production — Weekend Nights" or "Staging — Weekday Mornings". A clear name makes it easy to identify when assigning the window to environments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select days of the week">
    Check the days when maintenance is permitted. You can select any combination of days.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the time range">
    Enter a **Start time** and **End time** that define when maintenance can begin. Redwood will only start an upgrade within this time range.

    The time range must be long enough to accommodate the expected maintenance duration for the upgrades you plan to run. Check the estimated duration on upgrade details before setting a tight window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a timezone">
    Select the timezone that matches your operational hours. All times in the window configuration are interpreted in this timezone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the window">
    Click **Save**. The window is created and is now available to assign to environments.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Create at least two upgrade windows — one for production environments with strict off-peak timing, and one for non-production environments with more flexibility. This lets you test upgrades in staging before they reach production.
</Tip>

## Assign an upgrade window to an environment

After creating a window, assign it to one or more environments:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open environment settings">
    Navigate to **Environments**, select the environment you want to configure, and open its **Settings** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the upgrade window field">
    Scroll to the **Maintenance** section and locate the **Upgrade Window** field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a window">
    Choose the upgrade window you want to assign from the dropdown. The portal shows the window's days and time range so you can confirm the right one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save changes">
    Click **Save**. The selected window is now active for this environment. Any future upgrades scheduled for this environment will respect the window configuration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Edit an existing upgrade window

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Upgrade Windows">
    Go to **Upgrades** and select the **Upgrade Windows** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the window to edit">
    Click the name of the window you want to modify.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the configuration">
    Change the days, time range, or timezone as needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save**. Changes apply immediately to all environments using this window.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Editing an upgrade window affects all environments it's assigned to. If you have upgrades already scheduled for those environments, the scheduled time may shift to align with the updated window. Review any scheduled upgrades after making changes.
</Warning>

## Delete an upgrade window

To delete a window, open it from the **Upgrade Windows** tab and click **Delete**. You cannot delete a window that is currently assigned to one or more environments. Remove the window from all environments first, then delete it.

## How upgrade windows interact with scheduled upgrades

When you schedule an upgrade and choose a preferred date:

1. The portal looks for the next occurrence of your upgrade window on or after that date.
2. The upgrade is scheduled to begin at the start of the window on that day.
3. If no upgrade window is assigned to the environment, the upgrade runs at the default time on your chosen date.

If your preferred date falls within an upgrade window's allowed days and time range, the upgrade may be scheduled for that same day. If not, it moves forward to the next occurrence.

<Note>
  The portal always shows you the exact scheduled time before you confirm. You can adjust your preferred date if the calculated time doesn't work for your team.
</Note>

## Best practices

**Use off-peak hours.** Schedule windows during times when usage is lowest — typically late nights or weekends. This minimizes the impact on users if maintenance takes longer than expected.

**Account for upgrade duration.** Major releases can take longer than patches. Set your window end time with enough buffer so that even longer-running upgrades complete before business hours begin.

**Align across environments.** Stagger your production and staging windows so upgrades reach staging first. For example, schedule staging for Saturday night and production for Sunday night — giving you time to verify the upgrade in staging before it reaches production.

**Avoid financial and operational peaks.** For Finance Automation environments especially, avoid scheduling windows during month-end close, quarter-end reporting, or other high-activity periods.

**Account for timezones in global organizations.** If your users span multiple timezones, pick a window that falls outside peak hours in all major regions, or accept a short impact window for one region.

<Tip>
  Document your upgrade window schedule and share it with your team and end users so they know when to expect maintenance. Even brief, planned downtime is easier to manage when it's predictable.
</Tip>
