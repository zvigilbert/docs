When Redwood makes an upgrade available for your environment, you choose when to apply it. Scheduling through the portal lets you pick a date that works for your operations, confirm the maintenance window, and track the upgrade through to completion — all without involving Redwood support.

## View available upgrades

Navigate to **Upgrades** in the left sidebar to see all upgrades available across your environments. The list shows:

- **Environment name** — which environment the upgrade applies to
- **Current version** — the version currently running
- **Available version** — the version you can upgrade to
- **Upgrade type** — major release, minor update, or patch
- **Deadline** — for mandatory upgrades, the date by which you must schedule
- **Status** — available, scheduled, or in progress

Click any upgrade in the list to view release notes and details before deciding to schedule.

<Note>
  If no upgrades are listed, your environments are running the current version. Redwood will notify you when new upgrades become available.
</Note>

## Schedule an upgrade

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the upgrade">
    In the **Upgrades** section, find the environment you want to upgrade and click **Schedule** next to the available upgrade.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the upgrade details">
    Read the upgrade summary, including the version number, estimated maintenance duration, and any pre-upgrade requirements. Click the release notes link to review what's changing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a date">
    Choose your preferred upgrade date using the date picker. The system will schedule the upgrade to run during the first available upgrade window on or after the date you select.

    If no upgrade window is configured for this environment, the upgrade will run at the start of the maintenance period on your chosen date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the scheduled time">
    The portal displays the exact date and time the upgrade will run based on your selected date and the environment's upgrade window. Review this carefully before confirming.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the schedule">
    Click **Confirm Schedule**. The upgrade status changes to **Scheduled** and a confirmation email is sent to environment administrators.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Once an upgrade is scheduled, your environment will enter a maintenance state at the confirmed time. Make sure you've communicated the maintenance window to any users or downstream systems that depend on that environment.
</Warning>

## Reschedule an upgrade

If your plans change, you can reschedule a confirmed upgrade up until a cutoff period before the upgrade is set to begin.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the scheduled upgrade">
    In the **Upgrades** section, locate the environment with a **Scheduled** status and click on it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Reschedule">
    On the upgrade details page, click **Reschedule**. This cancels the current schedule without canceling the upgrade itself.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a new date">
    Pick a new preferred date. The portal will calculate a new scheduled time based on your upgrade window configuration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the new schedule">
    Review the updated scheduled time and click **Confirm Schedule** to save the change.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You cannot reschedule a mandatory upgrade past its deadline. If the new date you select would push the upgrade past the deadline, the portal will alert you.
</Note>

## Cancel a scheduled upgrade

You can cancel an optional upgrade at any time before it begins. Mandatory upgrades cannot be permanently canceled — only rescheduled within the allowed window.

To cancel, open the scheduled upgrade in the **Upgrades** section and click **Cancel Schedule**. The upgrade returns to **Available** status and can be rescheduled later.

## What happens during the upgrade

Once the scheduled time arrives:

1. The portal updates the upgrade status to **In Progress**.
2. The environment enters a maintenance state. Users attempting to access the environment will see a maintenance message.
3. Redwood applies the upgrade to the environment.
4. Automated health checks confirm the upgrade completed successfully.
5. The environment returns to service and the status changes to **Complete**.
6. Administrators receive a completion notification by email.

If an upgrade fails at any point, Redwood rolls back the environment to its previous version and alerts you immediately. The upgrade status shows **Failed** and includes details about what went wrong.

<Warning>
  Do not attempt to access or use the environment while an upgrade is in progress. Wait for the completion notification before resuming normal operations.
</Warning>

## Track upgrade history

After an upgrade completes, it moves to the **Upgrade History** tab in the **Upgrades** section. Each entry shows the version applied, the date and duration of maintenance, and the outcome. Use upgrade history to audit your environments and verify which version each environment is running.
