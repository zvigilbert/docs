Single Sign-On (SSO) lets your team members authenticate to the Redwood Cloud Portal using your organization's existing identity provider (IdP) rather than managing a separate portal password. Once SSO is enabled, users sign in through your IdP — benefiting from your organization's existing password policies, multi-factor authentication enforcement, and centralized access control. For organizations with strict security requirements or large teams, SSO is the recommended authentication method.

## Supported identity providers

The Redwood Cloud Portal supports any identity provider that implements **SAML 2.0** or **OpenID Connect (OIDC)**. Commonly used providers include:

- **Okta**
- **Microsoft Azure Active Directory (Azure AD / Entra ID)**
- **Google Workspace**
- **OneLogin**
- **Ping Identity**
- **Auth0**
- Any other SAML 2.0 or OIDC-compliant provider

<Note>
  Contact [Redwood Support](https://portal.redwood.cloud/) if you are unsure whether your identity provider is compatible.
</Note>

## Prerequisites

Before you configure SSO, confirm the following:

- You have the **Administrator** role in the Redwood Cloud Portal.
- You have administrative access to your organization's identity provider.
- Your IdP supports SAML 2.0 or OIDC.
- You have the following information available from your IdP:
  - **SAML:** IdP metadata XML file or metadata URL, Single Sign-On URL, and X.509 certificate.
  - **OIDC:** Issuer URL, Client ID, and Client Secret.

## Configure SSO in the portal

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="SAML 2.0">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to SSO settings">
        In the left navigation sidebar, select **Users & Access**, then select **Single Sign-On**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select SAML 2.0">
        On the SSO configuration page, select **SAML 2.0** as the protocol.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Retrieve your Service Provider details">
        The portal displays your **SP Entity ID** and **ACS (Assertion Consumer Service) URL**. Copy these values — you will need them when creating the application in your IdP.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create an application in your IdP">
        In your identity provider's admin console, create a new SAML application using the SP Entity ID and ACS URL from the previous step. Configure your IdP to send the user's email address as the **NameID** attribute.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter your IdP metadata in the portal">
        Return to the portal's SSO settings page. Provide your IdP configuration using one of these methods:

        - **Metadata URL** — Paste the URL where your IdP hosts its SAML metadata. The portal fetches and stores the configuration automatically.
        - **Metadata XML** — Upload the metadata XML file you downloaded from your IdP.

        Alternatively, enter the values manually:
        - **IdP Entity ID**
        - **Single Sign-On URL**
        - **X.509 certificate**
      </Step>
      <Step title="Test the connection">
        Select **Test SSO Connection**. The portal opens a new browser tab and initiates a test sign-in through your IdP. Confirm that the test completes successfully before enabling SSO for your organization.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enable SSO">
        After a successful test, select **Enable SSO**. Users in your organization can now sign in using your IdP.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="OIDC">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to SSO settings">
        In the left navigation sidebar, select **Users & Access**, then select **Single Sign-On**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select OIDC">
        On the SSO configuration page, select **OpenID Connect (OIDC)** as the protocol.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Retrieve your redirect URI">
        The portal displays your **Redirect URI**. Copy this value — you will need it when registering the application in your IdP.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Register an application in your IdP">
        In your identity provider's admin console, register a new OIDC application. Set the **Redirect URI** to the value from the previous step. Note the **Client ID** and **Client Secret** generated by your IdP.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter your IdP details in the portal">
        Return to the portal's SSO settings page and enter:

        - **Issuer URL** — The base URL of your IdP's OIDC discovery endpoint.
        - **Client ID**
        - **Client Secret**
      </Step>
      <Step title="Test the connection">
        Select **Test SSO Connection**. The portal initiates a test authentication through your IdP in a new browser tab. Confirm the test completes successfully before proceeding.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enable SSO">
        After a successful test, select **Enable SSO**. Users in your organization can now sign in using your IdP.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  Do not close the SSO configuration page until you have successfully tested the connection. Enabling a misconfigured SSO can prevent users — including yourself — from signing in to the portal.
</Warning>

## How users sign in with SSO

Once SSO is enabled:

1. Users navigate to [portal.redwood.cloud](https://portal.redwood.cloud/).
2. On the sign-in page, they enter their **email address** and select **Continue**.
3. The portal detects that SSO is enabled for your organization's domain and redirects the user to your identity provider.
4. The user authenticates through your IdP (using your organization's credentials and any MFA requirements you have configured).
5. On successful authentication, the IdP redirects the user back to the portal and they are signed in.

<Note>
  Users with existing portal-managed passwords can continue to sign in with their password if SSO is configured but not set as mandatory. Contact Redwood Support if you want to enforce SSO for all users and disable password-based login.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The test connection fails with an 'Invalid ACS URL' error">
    Verify that the **ACS URL** entered in your IdP exactly matches the value shown on the portal's SSO settings page, including the protocol (`https://`) and any trailing path. Even a single character difference will cause the assertion to be rejected.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Users are redirected to the IdP but land on an error page after authenticating">
    This usually indicates a **certificate mismatch** or an **expired X.509 certificate**. Download a fresh copy of your IdP's signing certificate and update it on the portal's SSO settings page. Also confirm that your IdP is sending the user's email as the NameID attribute.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Only some users can sign in via SSO">
    Confirm that those users are assigned to the SAML or OIDC application in your IdP. Users who are not assigned to the application in the IdP will be blocked at the IdP level, regardless of their status in the portal.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="SSO is enabled but users still see the password login form">
    The portal uses the email domain to detect which users should be redirected to SSO. Verify that your users' email addresses match the domain you registered during SSO setup. Users with email addresses on a different domain will see the standard login form.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The IdP metadata URL is not loading">
    Some IdPs restrict access to metadata endpoints by IP address or require authentication. If the portal cannot fetch your metadata URL automatically, download the metadata XML from your IdP and upload the file directly instead.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  Most SSO issues originate in the IdP configuration rather than the portal. Use your IdP's built-in SAML/OIDC diagnostic tools to inspect the authentication request and response before escalating to Redwood Support.
</Tip>
