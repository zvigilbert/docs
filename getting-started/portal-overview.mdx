Once you sign in to the Redwood Cloud Portal, you have access to a centralized dashboard that covers every aspect of managing your Redwood SaaS products. The portal organizes its functionality into distinct sections, each focused on a specific management area — from connecting to product environments and administering users, to scheduling upgrades and reviewing consumption data.

## Main navigation areas

The portal's primary navigation is organized into the following sections. Select any section from the left sidebar to open it.

## Environments

The **Environments** section is the primary area for accessing your Redwood SaaS product instances. Each environment card shows the product name, environment type (such as production or non-production), and current status.

From **Environments**, you can:

- View all provisioned instances of RunMyJobs, Finance Automation, and JSCAPE.
- Connect directly to a product environment by selecting **Open** or the equivalent launch control on the environment card.
- Review environment-level details such as version, region, and status.

<Note>
  The environments listed here reflect what has been provisioned for your organization. If you expect to see an environment that is not listed, contact your Redwood account team.
</Note>

## Users & Roles

The **Users & Roles** section lets you control who has access to the portal and what they can do within it. Portal administrators can create new user accounts, update existing ones, and assign roles that define each user's level of access.

From **Users & Roles**, you can:

- Invite new users by entering their email address and assigning an initial role.
- Edit existing user profiles and update role assignments.
- Deactivate or remove users who no longer require access.
- Review which roles are available and what permissions each role grants.

Roles determine access at the portal level. Users with limited roles may only see specific sections or have read-only access to certain areas.

## Upgrades

The **Upgrades** section gives you visibility into scheduled product upgrades and lets you configure when upgrades are applied to your environments.

From **Upgrades**, you can:

- View upcoming upgrades for each of your environments.
- Configure maintenance windows that define the permitted time ranges for upgrades to be applied.
- Review the history of previously completed upgrades.

<Tip>
  Set maintenance windows that align with low-traffic periods for your organization to minimize the impact of upgrade activity on your users.
</Tip>

## Monitoring

The **Monitoring** section provides an audit trail of activity across your portal account and connected environments.

From **Monitoring**, you can:

- Browse the activity log to see a record of user actions and automated events.
- Filter activity by user, event type, or date range.
- Review and manage notification settings for your environments.

Use the activity log to investigate unexpected changes or to meet audit and compliance requirements.

## Consumption & Contracts

The **Consumption & Contracts** section gives you a view of your organization's product usage relative to your active contracts with Redwood.

From **Consumption & Contracts**, you can:

- Review current usage metrics for your active SaaS products.
- View the terms and entitlements of your contracts.
- Track consumption trends over time.

<Note>
  If you have questions about your contract or believe your consumption data is incorrect, contact your Redwood account team or email [docsupport@redwood.com](mailto:docsupport@redwood.com).
</Note>

## Switch between sections

Select any section name in the left sidebar to navigate directly to it. The sidebar is always visible, so you can move between sections at any point without losing your place.

If you are working inside a specific environment or a detailed view, use the breadcrumb trail at the top of the page or the sidebar to return to a higher-level section.
