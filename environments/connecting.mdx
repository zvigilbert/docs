The Redwood Cloud Portal is the starting point for reaching any of your product instances. Instead of bookmarking separate URLs for each environment, you connect to RunMyJobs, Finance Automation, and JSCAPE directly from the portal's **Environments** dashboard — one place for your entire Redwood footprint.

## Find your environments

All environments provisioned for your organization appear on the **Environments** page. You can reach it from the left navigation menu.

The list shows every environment your account can access. If you manage multiple products or multiple tiers (production and non-production), they all appear here. Use the product and type filters at the top of the list to narrow down the view when you have many environments.

<Tip>
  Bookmark the Environments page directly rather than individual environment URLs. Environment URLs can change after upgrades or infrastructure events, but the portal link remains stable.
</Tip>

## Connect to an environment

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to the Redwood Cloud Portal">
    Navigate to [portal.redwood.cloud](https://portal.redwood.cloud/) and sign in with your credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Environments page">
    In the left navigation, select **Environments**. The dashboard lists all environments available to your organization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate the environment you want">
    Find the environment by name, product type, or tier. Confirm the **Status** column shows **Active** before attempting to connect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the environment">
    Click the environment name or the **Open** button next to it. The portal redirects you to the environment's login page or directly into the product interface, depending on how your organization has configured access.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Open an environment in a new tab

If you want to keep the portal open alongside your product environment, right-click the **Open** button and select **Open in new tab** (or use your browser's equivalent shortcut). The portal session remains active in its original tab.

<Note>
  Some browsers block pop-ups from the portal. If clicking **Open** does not launch the environment, check your browser's pop-up blocker settings and allow pop-ups from `portal.redwood.cloud`.
</Note>

## What happens after you connect

What you see after clicking through depends on the product:

- **RunMyJobs** — You land on the RunMyJobs Operations Console, where you can monitor jobs, manage definitions, and review execution history.
- **Finance Automation** — You land on the Finance Automation workspace, where you can manage financial close processes, tasks, and reporting.
- **JSCAPE** — You land on the JSCAPE management console, where you can manage servers, connectors, users, and file transfer activity.

If your organization has configured Single Sign-On (SSO), you may be signed in automatically without a separate prompt. If SSO is not configured, you will sign in using your product-specific credentials.

## If an environment is unavailable

<Warning>
  Do not attempt to connect to an environment that shows a **Maintenance**, **Degraded**, or **Unavailable** status. Connecting during maintenance can produce unexpected errors, and degraded environments may not behave normally.
</Warning>

If the environment you need is not available:

1. Check the **Status** column on the Environments page for context on the current state.
2. Review any notifications or announcements in the portal for information about ongoing incidents or scheduled maintenance.
3. If the status is **Unavailable** with no explanation, or if a **Degraded** environment is affecting your business, contact Redwood Support through the portal or at your organization's designated support channel.

If you see an error page after clicking through to an environment — such as a 502 or connection timeout — return to the portal and check the environment status. The issue may have started after you clicked the link.
