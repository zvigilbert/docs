Environments are your individual instances of Redwood SaaS products — each one is a fully isolated deployment of RunMyJobs, Finance Automation, or JSCAPE that your organization uses for a specific purpose. The Redwood Cloud Portal gives you a single place to see all of your environments at once, check their status, and launch them without hunting for separate URLs or credentials.

## What an environment is

Each environment corresponds to one running instance of a Redwood product. When Redwood provisions your organization's subscription, it creates one or more environments depending on your contract. A typical organization has at least two environments per product: one for production workloads and one for testing or staging.

Environments are independent of each other. Changes you make in a staging environment — such as testing a new job schedule or file transfer configuration — do not affect your production environment until you intentionally promote or replicate them.

<Note>
  The number and type of environments available to your organization depends on your Redwood contract. Contact your Redwood account team if you need additional environments provisioned.
</Note>

## The three Redwood SaaS products

Your environments will be instances of one or more of the following products:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="RunMyJobs" icon="calendar-check">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Finance Automation" icon="chart-line">

  <Card title="JSCAPE" icon="arrow-right-arrow-left">

  </Card>

## Multiple environments per product

Most organizations maintain separate environments for different stages of the software lifecycle:

- **Production** — Live systems serving real business workloads. Treat changes here carefully.
- **Staging / UAT** — Pre-production environments used to validate configurations, integrations, and upgrades before they reach production.
- **Development / Test** — Sandboxes for experimenting with new configurations without risk to production data or processes.

The portal displays all of your environments together regardless of product or type, so you can manage your entire Redwood footprint from one dashboard.

## The environments dashboard

When you navigate to **Environments** in the portal, you see a list of every environment provisioned for your organization. For each environment, the dashboard displays:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Name** | The display name of the environment, typically indicating the product and purpose (for example, "RunMyJobs Production"). |
| **Product** | The Redwood product the environment runs: RunMyJobs, Finance Automation, or JSCAPE. |
| **Type** | The environment tier, such as Production or Non-Production. |
| **Region** | The cloud region or data center where the environment is hosted. |
| **Status** | The current operational state of the environment (see below). |

## Environment status indicators

Each environment displays a status badge that reflects its current health and availability:

- **Active** — The environment is running normally and available to connect to.
- **Maintenance** — The environment is temporarily unavailable while Redwood performs scheduled maintenance or an upgrade.
- **Degraded** — The environment is running but experiencing reduced performance or partial unavailability. Redwood support is typically already investigating.
- **Unavailable** — The environment cannot be reached. This may be caused by an incident or a provisioning issue.

<Info>
  If an environment shows a **Degraded** or **Unavailable** status and you have not received a notification from Redwood, open a support case so the team can investigate.
</Info>
