The Redwood Cloud Portal sends notifications to keep you informed about events that affect your environments and account—from upcoming scheduled upgrades to user management changes. Understanding how notifications work lets you stay on top of what matters without being overwhelmed by noise.

## Types of notifications

The portal generates notifications for the following categories of events:

- **Upgrade announcements** — Upcoming scheduled upgrades, changes to upgrade windows, and upgrade completion confirmations
- **Environment alerts** — Status changes, connectivity issues, or configuration changes affecting your environments
- **User changes** — New users added, roles modified, users removed, or SSO configuration updated
- **System maintenance** — Planned maintenance windows and any unplanned disruptions to portal services
- **Account changes** — Updates to contract details, consumption thresholds, or billing-related information

<Info>
  Notifications reflect portal-level events. For alerts about activity inside your product environments—such as job failures in RunMyJobs—configure alerting within that product directly.
</Info>

## View your notifications

Notifications appear in two places: the in-portal notification center and, if configured, your email inbox.

### In-portal notifications

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the notification center">
    Click the **bell icon** in the top-right corner of the portal header. A badge on the icon shows the count of unread notifications.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the notification list">
    The panel displays your most recent notifications, newest first. Each entry shows the event type, a brief description, and the time it occurred.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View notification details">
    Click any notification to see the full details and, where applicable, navigate directly to the affected resource.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Mark notifications as read or dismiss them

To keep your notification center manageable:

- **Mark as read** — Click the checkmark next to a notification, or open it to mark it as read automatically. Read notifications remain visible but are no longer counted in the unread badge.
- **Mark all as read** — Select **Mark all as read** at the top of the notification panel to clear the unread count in one action.
- **Dismiss a notification** — Click the **X** next to an individual notification to remove it from your list permanently.
- **Dismiss all** — Select **Dismiss all** to clear all notifications from your panel at once.

<Note>
  Dismissing a notification removes it from your view only. The underlying event is still captured in the activity log.
</Note>

## Configure notification preferences

You can control which events generate notifications and how you receive them.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to your account settings">
    Click your **profile icon** in the top-right corner and select **Notification Preferences**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review notification categories">
    The preferences page lists each notification category. For each one, you can enable or disable:
    - **In-portal** — Notifications that appear in the bell icon panel
    - **Email** — Notifications delivered to your account email address
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust your preferences">
    Toggle the switches to enable or disable each combination of category and delivery channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save preferences**. Changes take effect immediately for future events.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you receive notifications for events that don't require your attention, disable that category rather than dismissing notifications repeatedly. This keeps your notification center focused on what is actionable for you.
</Tip>

## Notification delivery by role

Not all notifications go to all users. The portal routes notifications based on your assigned role:

| Role | Notifications received |
|---|---|
| **Administrator** | All categories, including user changes, upgrades, environment alerts, and system maintenance |
| **Operator** | Environment alerts, upgrade announcements, and system maintenance |
  | **Viewer** | System maintenance and any notifications that apply to the whole account |

<Info>
  Administrators can review and modify the role-based notification routing for their organization under **Settings > Notification Routing**.
</Info>

## Subscribe and unsubscribe from notification types

To subscribe or unsubscribe from a specific notification category without changing your other preferences:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Notification Preferences">
    Navigate to your **profile icon** and select **Notification Preferences**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate the notification type">
    Find the category you want to change in the list—for example, **Upgrade Announcements** or **Environment Alerts**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle the delivery channels">
    Turn off both **In-portal** and **Email** for a category to unsubscribe from it entirely. Turn on either channel to subscribe.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your preferences">
    Click **Save preferences** to apply the change.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Some notifications—such as critical system maintenance alerts—cannot be disabled. These are delivered regardless of your preferences to ensure you receive information that may require action.
</Note>
